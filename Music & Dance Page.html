Traditional Basotho Music and Dance

Traditional Dances

Mohobelo

Men's dance, showing strength, striding/leaping/sliding.

Ntlhlamo/Ndlamo

The dance was introduced to Basotho culture by the Matebele(Zulu-speaking people) and is performed by men and boys at ceremonies and public events. While indlamu is a Zulu dance known for high kicking and stomping, the Basotho version is distinguished by its heavy drum accompaniment, fast tempo, and coordinated foot stomping.

Mokorotlo

Perfomed by men for the chief on special occations; slow stamping and ceremonial moves.

Litolobonya

A form of song and dance exclusively for girls and married women. The girls' version of litolobonya is performed in the open, where everyone may watch, but the version for women is highly secretive and is performed only by those who have given live birth.

Mokhibo

Women's dance; rhythmic shoulder movements, often on knees, graceful.

Maqekha

Part of girls' initiation rites; special dance for that ceremony.

Pina tsa mokopu

Is a Basotho celebration and harvest festival song and dance for girls, where they dance in a cirle singing, with hand and foot movement. Involves rhythmic stamping and the use of bells and grass skirts for women.

Traditional Instruments

Sekhankula

A bowed monochord(one-sting instument), using a tin can resonator and wire with a bow

Lesiba

A string-and-wind instrument: one sting stretched over a wooden stick with a quill; played via inhalation/exhalation and mouth resonance.

Thomo

A stringed instrument; played by women traditionally.

Lekolulo

A kind of flute, played by herding boys.

Setolo-tolo

Mouth-bow type or mouth-resonated bow instrument, played by men using a mouth.

Lekope

A type of mouth bow(also called mouth-resonated), used by women; variation of SETOLO-TOLO among Basotho.

Moropa

A small drum(hand-sounded), used in girls' initiation songs and women's dancing.

Sekupu

Another type of drum, used by healers(mathuela) and in celebrations, often by women.

Sevuvu

A "bullroarer" type instrument(hand-sounded).

Morutlhoana

Shaken rattles.

Setjoli

Rubbed rattles.

Manyenenyene

Metal bells.

Sekebeku

A kind of idiophone, listed among endangered traditional instruments.

Liphala

Megaphone-type instruments(mouth-sounded, for amplifying or projecting voice/sound).

Phalana

Animal-horn trumpets.
